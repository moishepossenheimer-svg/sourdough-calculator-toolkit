Starter care

Sourdough Starter Maintenance Guide for Reliable Baking

A starter does not need perfection, but it does need a predictable rhythm. This guide explains how to keep it active without wasting flour.

Updated June 2026Original guide with professional references

What a healthy starter looks like

A healthy starter rises after feeding, shows bubbles through the side of the jar, smells pleasantly fruity, yogurty or lightly acidic, and eventually falls when it runs out of food. The exact timing depends on temperature, flour and feeding ratio.

Daily counter maintenance

If you bake often, keep a small starter at room temperature and feed it every day. A simple maintenance feed is 10g starter, 20g flour and 20g water. That is a 1:2:2 feed, which is enough food for many normal kitchens without creating too much discard.

Fridge maintenance

If you bake weekly or less, store the starter in the fridge after it has shown activity. Feed it, let it begin rising, then refrigerate. Refresh it weekly if possible. Before baking, give it one or two room-temperature feeds until it rises reliably again.

How to revive a sluggish starter

Keep only a small spoonful of starter, then feed 1:2:2 or 1:3:3 with fresh flour and water. Keep it warm, around normal room temperature or slightly warmer, and repeat for several cycles. Whole rye or whole wheat flour can help because it brings minerals and nutrients.

When to throw starter away

Starter is resilient, but fuzzy mould, pink streaks or orange streaks mean it should be discarded. Grey liquid is usually hooch, which means hunger rather than danger. Pour it off and feed. Strong unpleasant rot smells are also a reason to restart.

Reduce discard without neglecting the starter

Keeping less starter is the easiest way to reduce waste. You do not need a huge jar unless you are preparing for a bake. Maintain 20-50g total starter, then build a levain when you need more.

Frequently asked questions

How often should I feed my starter?

Feed daily if it lives at room temperature. If it lives in the fridge, feed weekly or refresh it before baking.

Can I bake with starter straight from the fridge?

It is better to refresh it first. Cold, unfed starter is usually slower and less predictable.

What flour is best for starter?

Bread flour works well for maintenance. Whole wheat or rye can help revive a sluggish starter.
